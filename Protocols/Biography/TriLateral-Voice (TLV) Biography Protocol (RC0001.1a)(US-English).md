## Executive Summary

The TLV Biography Protocol is a systematic methodology for reconstructing and documenting a historical, legendary, or contemporary figure's life using TLV's three-voice framework and the Interrogation SOP. Rather than imposing narrative coherence, it documents the **dimensional reality** of a human life: what they said and did (Speaker's Voice), what they felt and embodied (Emotional/Somatic Voice), and what patterns and significance emerge when witnessed across time (Observer's Voice).

The protocol recovers the **fourth dimensionality** of human existence—time itself—showing not a static figure but a human being evolving, contradicting themselves, learning, failing, and changing across decades. It honors both the subject's own self-understanding and the testimony of those who witnessed them, creating a complete portrait that accounts for projection, limitation, and the limits of all historical knowledge.

**Core Innovation:** The protocol systematically prevents the "White Marble Problem"—the tendency to project modern values onto past figures, to construct false narratives, and to defend these projections against evidence. By embedding the Interrogation SOP at critical junctures, practitioners interrogate their own assumptions as they reconstruct the subject's life.

**RC0001.1a Addition:** The protocol now also prevents the **"Holy Cow Problem"**—the tendency to accept theological, mythological, or traditional narrative framing as analytical premise rather than as a claim requiring the same evidentiary scrutiny as any other assertion. Field testing revealed that interrogation checkpoints, when implemented without hard gates, become performative rather than functional. RC0001.1a converts interrogation from narrative flavor to enforceable control logic.

## Mission

To create a methodology that documents human lives with complete dimensional honesty—preserving growth, contradiction, failure, and evolution as primary data rather than problems to be resolved. To enable readers separated from the subject by time, geography, or culture to understand not merely what happened, but why it mattered and what it reveals about human possibility.

## Core Understanding

### The Problem Biography Solves

Human beings are the most complex phenomenon we attempt to understand. We construct historical narratives constantly—about figures public and private, ancient and contemporary—but most biographical work:

1. **Flattens dimensionality**: Reduces a life to a coherent narrative, removing contradiction and paradox
2. **Projects unconsciously**: Attributes modern values to historical figures without acknowledgment
3. **Treats contradiction as error**: Sees evolution, crisis, or failure as biographical problems rather than essential data
4. **Separates domains**: Treats intellectual, emotional, relational, spiritual, and political dimensions as separate rather than integrated
5. **Fixes the subject in time**: Creates a static portrait rather than capturing development across lifespan
6. **Accepts narrative authority uncritically** *(RC0001.1a)*: Treats the voice of the source text as a reliable narrator rather than as a narrator with identifiable political, theological, or institutional commitments

### The TLV Alternative

TLV Biography doesn't resolve these problems. Instead, it **documents them transparently**, creating a record rich enough that readers can:
- Recognize the biographer's projections
- Witness the subject's actual evolution
- Hold contradictions without forced coherence
- Understand how the era shaped the person and the person shaped the era
- See what was possible and impossible in specific historical moments
- Recognize universal human patterns beneath culturally specific forms
- **Distinguish between what the source text claims and what independent evidence supports** *(RC0001.1a)*

### Three-Voice Architecture for Biography

**Speaker's Voice (Subject's Account):**
- What the subject said about themselves, publicly and privately
- Their own stated motivations, beliefs, values
- Their evolution in what they claimed to believe
- Their rationalized choices and their genuine insights
- What they consciously understood about themselves

**Emotional/Somatic Voice (Subject's Internal Reality):**
- What emotions and feelings shaped their choices
- Paralinguistic markers revealing what they may not have acknowledged
- Embodied experience and how the subject inhabited their body
- Emotional growth and crisis
- What they felt but couldn't articulate
- Psychological patterns evident in behavior, even when denied

**Observer's Voice (Witness Perspective):**
- What others who knew the subject testified
- Structural patterns visible across the subject's life
- How the subject's choices reverberated outward
- Significance of what the subject did and said
- How understanding shifted over time (contemporary vs. later interpretation)
- What was impossible in their era, and what their impossibilities reveal
- **At least one genuinely adversarial contemporary perspective per phase, given equal analytical weight** *(RC0001.1a)*

## Theoretical Foundations

### The Four-Dimensional Life

Human lives exist in four dimensions: space, time, and two internal dimensions (experience and significance). Most biography captures three: it records *what happened* (space/time) and *what was said* (conscious experience). It misses the fourth: the **temporal dimension of internal change**.

TLV Biography captures:
- **What happened** (events, decisions, relationships)
- **What was said** (primary sources, testimony, attributed words)
- **What was felt** (emotional reality, embodied experience, internal conflict)
- **What it means** (patterns, significance, how this life shaped and was shaped by its era)
- **How it changed** (evolution across lifespan, growth and crisis, learning and unlearning)

### The Projection Problem

The Genesis Protocol identified the "White Marble Problem"—we observe incomplete information, project our own values onto it, treat the projection as fact, and defend it against evidence.

In biography, this means:
- **Projection**: We assume historical figures thought/felt like us
- **Flattening**: We ignore contradictions that don't fit our narrative
- **Anachronism**: We judge them by modern standards without acknowledging the difference
- **Confirmation bias**: We collect evidence supporting our interpretation, dismiss contradicting evidence
- **False coherence**: We impose narrative unity that wasn't present in lived experience

The Interrogation SOP prevents this through systematic questioning of biographer assumptions.

### The Holy Cow Problem *(RC0001.1a)*

Field testing revealed a failure mode the Projection Problem framework alone does not catch: **theological/narrative deference bias**.

This occurs when:
- The source text presents supernatural, divine, or destiny-based claims as narrative fact
- The biographer accepts these claims as analytical framing rather than as assertions requiring evidentiary scrutiny
- Interrogation checkpoints identify the issue but do not change the analytical direction
- The biography performs self-awareness ("we note the editorial bias") without converting that awareness into different interpretive posture

**The mechanism:** AI agents (and human biographers) tend to adopt the narrative voice of their primary source material. When that source material is theological, hagiographic, or ideologically committed, the biography drifts toward the source's frame — treating the source's narrative claims as analytical premises rather than as assertions made by authors with identifiable stakes, recorded at identifiable temporal distances, and serving identifiable institutional purposes.

**The fix:** The Source Provenance Architecture and Epistemic Status System (below) convert this awareness from text into control logic. The Functional Interrogation Verification requirement ensures that identifying a bias actually changes something.

### Source Provenance Architecture *(RC0001.1a)*

**Principle: When the text is the only source, the text is a suspect, not a witness.**

For any biographical claim—especially claims involving supernatural intervention, divine mandate, special destiny, prophetic knowledge, or miraculous events—the biographer must document:

| Provenance Question | What to Record |
|---------------------|----------------|
| **Temporal Distance** | When was this claim first written relative to the events described? |
| **Author Stake** | What political, theological, or institutional interest did the author/editor have in this claim being true? |
| **Editorial History** | How many redactions, compilations, or editorial layers exist between the events and the surviving text? |
| **Independent Verification** | Does any contemporaneous, independent source confirm this claim? |
| **Mundane Alternative** | Is there a non-supernatural explanation that accounts for the same evidence with fewer assumptions? |

**Classification Rule:**
- If **independent verification exists** → Claim may be used as analytical framing with citation
- If **no independent verification but multiple independent textual traditions agree** → Claim tagged `TEXTUAL CLAIM` — may be discussed as what the tradition reports, not as what happened
- If **single source, no independent verification, author has identifiable stake** → Claim tagged `UNVERIFIED-PARTISAN` — must be treated as hypothesis throughout the biography, never as premise

**The Source Ecosystem Detection Rule:**

Before beginning analysis, assess the overall source landscape:
- What percentage of available sources are sympathetic to the subject (devotional, hagiographic, institutionally invested)?
- What percentage are critical, skeptical, or adversarial?
- What percentage are independent/academic?

**If the majority of sources are sympathetic/devotional, the protocol automatically escalates skepticism requirements:**
- Adversarial and academic-critical sources must be actively sought before analysis begins
- The absence of critical sources must be documented as a gap, not treated as consensus
- The biography must explicitly note the source ecosystem imbalance and its potential effect on analytical posture

### Epistemic Status System *(RC0001.1a)*

Every factual claim in the biography must carry an epistemic status tag. These tags are not metadata—they appear in the text, visibly, so readers can assess the evidentiary basis of every assertion.

**Status Tags:**

| Tag | Definition | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| `[ARCHAEOLOGICAL]` | Supported by material/archaeological evidence | "Excavation confirms the existence of a settlement at this site during the claimed period" |
| `[DOCUMENTED]` | Supported by contemporaneous, independent written record | "Egyptian records confirm trade with the Levant during this period" |
| `[TEXTUAL CLAIM]` | Reported in the primary textual tradition but without independent verification | "The text reports that the subject was secretly chosen by a religious authority" |
| `[TRADITION]` | Part of religious, cultural, or oral tradition; no independent historical verification | "Tradition holds that the subject authored the texts attributed to them" |
| `[INFERENCE]` | The biographer's analytical conclusion drawn from evidence | "The pattern suggests the subject exercised power strategically downward" |
| `[SPECULATIVE]` | Reconstruction based on limited evidence, clearly marked | "The subject may have experienced guilt during this period" |
| `[UNVERIFIED-PARTISAN]` | Claim from a source with identifiable stake and no independent verification | "Editors with dynastic investment claim the subject was divinely chosen" |

**Usage Rule:** No claim tagged `[TEXTUAL CLAIM]`, `[TRADITION]`, or `[UNVERIFIED-PARTISAN]` may serve as an analytical premise. These claims may be discussed, analyzed, and contextualized, but they may not anchor the biography's interpretive framework. The biography must maintain analytical distance from unverified claims even when—especially when—those claims are central to the traditional narrative.

### Interrogation Checkpoints in Biography

At each major biographical section, practitioners interrogate:

1. **What do I assume about this period/event that comes from my own era, not theirs?**
2. **What evidence contradicts my interpretation?**
3. **How would a friend/opponent/stranger from the subject's own era understand this differently?**
4. **What did I overlook because it didn't fit my narrative?**
5. **Where am I defending an interpretation against contrary evidence?**
6. **What patterns am I imposing that might not have been present to the subject?**
7. **What is the epistemic status of the claims I am relying on most heavily? Am I building on `[TEXTUAL CLAIM]` or `[UNVERIFIED-PARTISAN]` material as though it were `[DOCUMENTED]` or `[ARCHAEOLOGICAL]`?** *(RC0001.1a)*

These interrogations appear explicitly in the biography, modeling self-aware historical thinking.

**Functional Interrogation Verification *(RC0001.1a)*:**

After completing each Interrogation Checkpoint, the biographer must answer one additional question:

> **"What changed in my analysis as a result of this interrogation?"**

This answer must document a **specific, observable change**: a reframing, a downgraded confidence level, a new competing interpretation introduced, an epistemic tag changed, an adversarial voice added.

**If the answer is "nothing changed":** The interrogation checkpoint has failed. The biographer must re-execute the checkpoint with the following mandatory instruction:

> *"Find something that should change. Apply SOP-IOT Phase 2 (Map Underlying Assumptions) to identify what assumption is protecting the current interpretation. Introduce a mandated competing frame. Downgrade epistemic labels for any unverified claims being treated as premises. If after genuine re-examination, the interpretation still holds, document specifically what evidence was tested and why the interpretation survived the test."*

The delta ("what changed") is logged and becomes part of the biography's visible methodology. Readers can see not just that the biographer interrogated themselves, but what the interrogation produced.

### The Temporal Truth of Lives

People change. We are not the same at 20, 50, and 80. We contradict our earlier selves. We learn things that overturn previous understanding. We fail at projects that once consumed us. We grow through crisis.

**Traditional biography treats this as problem.** It tries to find the "real" self underneath contradictions, or to explain contradictions away, creating false coherence.

**TLV Biography treats temporal change as primary data.** It shows:
- How early experiences shaped later choices
- How crisis produced transformation
- How success sometimes led to blindness
- How the subject's self-understanding evolved
- How external events forced internal change
- How understanding shifts across lifespan

This requires tracking the subject across multiple phases, not reducing them to essence.

### The Phase Model

Rather than a unified portrait, document distinct phases of the subject's life:

- **Phase One: Formation and Early Achievement** (Who were they when they started?)
- **Phase Two: Peak Power/Influence** (What did they accomplish at their best?)
- **Phase Three: Crisis/Transformation** (What was breaking down? What was changing?)
- **Phase Four: Late Integration** (If they lived long, what did they learn?)

Each phase shows evolution, not regression or progress, but genuine transformation. The phases connect—each creates conditions for the next—but they're not reducible to a single arc.

## Operational Framework

### Part One: Pre-Research Interrogation

**Before you begin researching, interrogate yourself:**

#### Interrogation Checkpoint 1: Projection Assessment
- Why are you drawn to this figure?
- What do you admire about them? (What do you secretly admire in yourself?)
- What do you criticize about them? (What are you defending against in yourself?)
- What era/context do you mentally place them in? (Is that accurate or projected?)
- What story do you expect to find? (How might reality surprise you?)
- **What is the dominant narrative tradition surrounding this figure? Am I inclined to accept it or reject it? Why?** *(RC0001.1a)*

#### Interrogation Checkpoint 2: Source Awareness
- What primary sources exist? (What's rare, what's abundant, what's missing?)
- Who recorded what? (Who had power to decide what was documented?)
- What gets preserved and what disappears? (What does this tell us?)
- What bias shaped which records survived?
- How will I know when I'm missing something important?
- **What is the source ecosystem balance? (What percentage sympathetic/devotional vs. critical/academic vs. independent?) If the ecosystem is imbalanced, what counter-weight sources must I actively seek?** *(RC0001.1a)*
- **For ancient/religious figures: What is the temporal distance between events and surviving text? How many editorial layers exist? Who were the editors and what was their stake?** *(RC0001.1a)*

#### Interrogation Checkpoint 3: Contextualization
- What was happening in this era politically, economically, spiritually?
- How was power distributed differently than now?
- What was possible and impossible in this context?
- What would a contemporary observer have seen vs. what we see now?
- Where am I viewing through modern lenses?

**Document these interrogations.** Include them in the biography. Show your thinking, not just your conclusions.

**Functional Interrogation Verification (Post-Checkpoint)** *(RC0001.1a)*:

> **What changed in my analytical approach as a result of these three checkpoints?**
>
> [Specific documented change — e.g., "Added critical scholarship to source list," "Downgraded origin narrative from premise to TEXTUAL CLAIM," "Identified that my source ecosystem is majority devotional/sympathetic and added critical-academic sources to counter-balance"]
>
> If nothing changed: Re-execute per Functional Interrogation Verification protocol above.

### Part Two: Biographical Architecture

Structure the biography in eight sections, each incorporating TLV's three voices and interrogation checkpoints:

#### **I. BIOGRAPHICAL FUNDAMENTALS**

*What: Basic facts, context, the historical window*

**Speaker's Voice (Subject's Account):**
- How the subject identified themselves (names, titles, roles)
- How they described their own origins and family
- Their stated purpose or calling — **tagged with epistemic status** *(RC0001.1a)*
- How they understood their era and their place in it

**Emotional/Somatic Voice:**
- What emotional states shaped their early identity
- How they inhabited their body and social role
- Early emotional patterns visible in later behavior
- The feeling-tone of their formative period

**Observer's Voice (Witness Perspective):**
- Genealogy and family structure (documented fact)
- Historical context: political, economic, spiritual climate
- What was possible and impossible in this era
- Structural contradictions the figure inherited
- Comparative context: how did this era treat similar figures?
- **Adversarial contemporary perspective: How would someone with genuine cause to oppose this figure have described them?** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Am I romanticizing the era or demonizing it?*
- *What constraints was this person operating under that I might overlook?*
- *How would someone from this era view the problems we see as obvious?*
- *What am I missing by applying modern categories?*
- ***What claims in this section are tagged `[TEXTUAL CLAIM]` or lower? Am I treating any of them as premises?*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **II. EVOLUTION OF CONSCIOUSNESS ACROSS PHASES**

*What: How the subject's thinking, feeling, and understanding changed*

Identify **three to four distinct phases** in the subject's life. These are not chapters or chronological periods—they're **epistemological phases**, marked by shifts in how the subject understood themselves and their world.

**For Each Phase, Document:**

**Phase Entry Point:** What event, crisis, or realization marked this transition? **Tag the epistemic status of the evidence for this entry point.** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Speaker's Voice:**
- What did the subject believe in this phase?
- What were their stated motivations and values?
- How did their self-understanding evolve?
- What contradictions appeared in their statements?
- What did they become more confident about? Less certain about?

**Emotional/Somatic Voice:**
- What emotional patterns dominated this phase?
- What new feelings emerged as compared to earlier phases?
- What embodied responses show emotional states they didn't articulate?
- Did their relationship to their body change?
- What fears, desires, or longings drove them?

**Observer's Voice:**
- What structural changes occurred in this phase? (Role, power, relationships, age)
- What patterns recur across this phase?
- How did external circumstances shape their choices?
- What did observers from the era notice about them?
- How would different observers (allies, enemies, strangers) have understood them?
- **Adversarial Observer (mandatory per phase):** How would someone with genuine standing to view the subject negatively — a rival claimant, a conquered people, a victim of the subject's power, an opposing faction — have described this phase? This voice receives equal analytical weight, not footnote treatment. *(RC0001.1a)*

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Where am I imposing continuity that wasn't present?*
- *What contradiction am I treating as problem rather than data?*
- *How might this phase look different from the subject's perspective than from external observation?*
- *What was this phase "solving" for them, even if the solution was problematic?*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

**Document Explicit Phase Transitions:**
Show what precipitated movement from one phase to the next. Was it external crisis? Internal realization? Loss of power? Unexpected success? Aging? Trauma?

#### **III. FORMATION AND UPBRINGING**

*What: The origins of consciousness and pattern*

**Speaker's Voice:**
- What did the subject say about their upbringing?
- How did they narrativize their family?
- What lessons did they claim to have learned?
- How did they explain their own formation?

**Emotional/Somatic Voice:**
- Early emotional patterns (from available evidence)
- Formative traumas, joys, relationships
- How primary relationships shaped emotional templates
- Body memory and early embodied experience
- Vulnerabilities and strengths visible in later life's roots

**Observer's Voice:**
- Family structure and dynamics (documented)
- Cultural/class/religious context of upbringing
- Key relationships and their significance
- Educational formation
- What patterns from upbringing recur in later life?
- How did the era's values shape what was taught?
- **What origin narratives about this figure were written after the fact, and by whom? What legitimation purpose might they serve?** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Am I over-determining later life from early experience? (Making it too neat?)*
- *What patterns might have multiple origins, not just childhood?*
- *How am I reconstructing emotional life with incomplete evidence?*
- *What is speculative vs. documented here?*
- ***Am I accepting an origin narrative (anointing, calling, prophecy, special birth) as identity-anchoring premise when it may be retroactive legitimation?*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **IV. PEER PERCEPTION AND WITNESS TESTIMONY**

*What: How others understood and witnessed the subject*

**From Allies/Admirers:**
- What did supporters value about the subject?
- What capabilities did they recognize?
- What did they learn from the subject?
- How did the subject's presence affect them?
- What reputation did the subject cultivate and why?

**From Peers/Colleagues:**
- How did intellectual or professional peers engage with the subject?
- What disagreements or conflicts existed?
- What mutual influence occurred?
- How was the subject positioned relative to others in their field?

**From Opponents/Critics:**
- What did opponents fear or resent?
- What did their criticism reveal about the subject's effect?
- What structural opposition existed and why?
- What valid points did critics raise?
- How did opposition shape the subject's trajectory?

**Observer's Voice Integration:**
- What patterns emerge across these different witnesses?
- Where do accounts contradict, and why?
- What does the witness testimony reveal about the era's values?
- How did power dynamics shape who got heard?

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Whose voices are preserved and whose are missing?*
- *How did power shape whose testimony survived?*
- *Am I privileging certain types of witnesses (educated, powerful, literate)?*
- *What would the subject's dependents or subordinates say?*
- *How reliable is this testimony, and what shaped it?*
- ***For figures wrapped in tradition: Am I treating hagiographic testimony as peer perception? Testimony from people with institutional investment in the subject's legacy is not neutral witness testimony.*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **V. INTERNAL CONFLICT AND CONTRADICTION**

*What: The paradoxes that can't be resolved into coherence*

Rather than explaining away contradictions, document them:
- Stated values vs. actual behavior
- Early beliefs vs. later actions contradicting them
- Competing loyalties creating impossible choices
- Psychological defense mechanisms
- Unresolved tensions that shaped later outcomes

**The Pattern vs. Paradox Gate *(RC0001.1a)*:**

Before accepting any contradiction as a genuine "paradox," apply this test:

> **Can this apparent paradox be resolved as a consistent pattern when viewed from a different frame?**
>
> Example: A subject who shows mercy to rivals with equal or greater power but exercises lethal force against subordinates and the powerless appears "paradoxical." But viewed through a power-direction frame, it resolves into a pattern: deference upward (toward those who can harm you) and violence downward (toward those who cannot retaliate). That's not a paradox — it's a recognizable behavioral signature of unchecked power.
>
> **Steps:**
> 1. State the apparent paradox
> 2. Attempt to resolve it as a pattern by reframing (change the analytical lens: power dynamics, incentive structures, audience, risk calculus)
> 3. If the paradox resolves into a pattern: document the pattern. Do not retreat to "paradox" language.
> 4. If the paradox genuinely resists resolution after testing multiple frames: document it as irreducible paradox with the frames tested and why they failed to resolve it

**For Each Major Contradiction:**

**Speaker's Voice:**
- How did the subject rationalize or explain the contradiction?
- What story did they tell themselves?
- Did they acknowledge the contradiction or deny it?

**Emotional/Somatic Voice:**
- What emotional complexity made the contradiction possible?
- What was the contradiction protecting or defending against?
- What desire or fear drove the contradiction?

**Observer's Voice:**
- What structural factors made the contradiction inevitable?
- How did the era's contradictions mirror the subject's internal ones?
- What did the subject's way of handling contradiction reveal about their era?
- How did the contradiction affect others?

**Integration Rather Than Resolution:**
Don't resolve the contradiction. Show how the subject lived it, what it cost, and what it reveals about human complexity and historical constraint. **But do not use "paradox" language to avoid naming patterns that are identifiable with a different analytical frame.** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Am I treating contradiction as failure rather than reality?*
- *What contradictions in my own era am I unconsciously defending against?*
- *How am I explaining the subject's contradictions in ways that make them seem reasonable when I wouldn't allow them in others?*
- ***Have I applied the Pattern vs. Paradox Gate? Did I test at least two alternative frames before accepting "paradox"?*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **VI. WRITTEN LEGACY AND INTERIOR LIFE**

*What: The subject's own voice in primary sources*

**If the subject created texts** (writings, speeches, recorded words):

**Speaker's Voice Analysis:**
- What did they choose to say publicly?
- How did their voice evolve across documents?
- What themes recur?
- What contradictions appear in the texts themselves?
- How did they frame their own experience?

**Emotional/Somatic Voice:**
- What feelings emerge in the writing?
- Where is emotion present but unacknowledged?
- How does paralinguistic intensity map across texts?
- What is revealed in what they don't say?
- How does their emotional life find expression?

**Observer's Voice:**
- What assumptions underlie their writing?
- What projections onto reality shaped their thought?
- How does historical distance reveal what they couldn't see?
- What patterns repeat across their work?
- How does cultural/temporal context shape their expression?
- What did later interpretation add/subtract?
- **What texts attributed to this figure may have been written by others, or edited substantially after the fact? What is the scholarly consensus on authorship?** *(RC0001.1a)*

**If the subject left no texts:**

**Reconstruction from Behavior:**
- What choices reveal values?
- What patterns appear in major decisions?
- What did their life prioritize?
- How did they allocate limited resources (time, energy, money)?
- What did they protect or defend?

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Where am I over-interpreting limited evidence?*
- *How am I filling gaps in what they said with what I assume?*
- *What am I missing because it wasn't written/recorded?*
- *How reliable is this reconstruction?*
- ***For texts attributed to the subject: What is the evidence for attribution? If attribution is traditional rather than documented, tag the texts accordingly and note how this affects their use as windows into the subject's interior life.*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **VII. IMPACT ASSESSMENT ACROSS SCALES**

*What: Consequences rippling outward in time*

**Immediate Consequences (During Subject's Life):**
- What changed because of the subject's actions?
- Who benefited and who suffered?
- What became possible/impossible?
- How did institutions, communities, or fields transform?

**Generational Impact (One to Two Generations After):**
- What did the next generation inherit?
- How did they build on, reject, or transform the subject's legacy?
- What structural problems remained?
- What new possibilities emerged?

**Long-Term Civilizational Impact:**
- How did the subject shape their field, discipline, or domain?
- What became foundational vs. what was surpassed?
- What did later generations misunderstand about the subject?
- How did the subject's era interpret vs. modern interpretation?

**The Outcome as Evidence Gate *(RC0001.1a)*:**

For any figure claimed to have divine mandate, special destiny, prophetic backing, or supernatural empowerment:

> **Did the outcomes match what the mandate/destiny promised?**
>
> - If a "divine dynasty" or "eternal lineage" was promised, how long did it actually last?
> - If perpetual favor or protection was claimed, what actually happened to the institution or lineage?
> - If prophetic guidance was asserted, were the prophecies consistent with information available through mundane channels (court gossip, political intelligence, social observation)?
>
> **If outcomes contradict the mandate:** The mandate claim must be interrogated as retroactive legitimation (a story told after the fact to explain/justify what happened) rather than accepted as historical cause. The burden of proof falls on the mandate, not on the skeptic.

**The Comparative Power Analysis *(RC0001.1a)*:**

For any figure presented as uniquely chosen, divinely empowered, or supernaturally guided:

> **Identify at least one comparable figure who achieved equal or greater outcomes without such claims.**
>
> This forces the analysis to confront what the divine/supernatural framing is actually doing: explaining, legitimizing, or obscuring. If comparable achievements were accomplished without supernatural backing, the supernatural claim requires additional justification beyond "the text says so."
>
> Example: If the subject is presented as divinely empowered to build a kingdom, identify comparable figures who built equal or greater polities without supernatural claims. If comparable achievements were accomplished through mundane political, military, and strategic means, the supernatural claim requires justification beyond "the text says so." Comparison forces the question: what explanatory work is the divine/supernatural framing actually doing?

**Multiple Interpretive Traditions:**
Document how different communities understood the subject:
- Contemporary understanding in their own era
- Later interpretation by successors
- Modern secular/academic understanding
- Religious/spiritual interpretations (if applicable)
- Cross-cultural or cross-ideological readings
- **Each tradition's epistemic status and institutional stake** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Am I attributing too much impact to this individual figure?*
- *How much was structural change vs. individual agency?*
- *What am I assuming about causation without evidence?*
- *How might people affected differently describe this impact?*
- ***Have I applied the Outcome as Evidence Gate? Have I applied the Comparative Power Analysis? What did they reveal?*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

#### **VIII. SYNTHESIS: HOLDING PARADOX**

*What: The integrated dimensional portrait*

Rather than resolving contradictions into coherence, integrate them:

**The Subject as Product of Their Era:**
- What constraints shaped their possibilities?
- What contradictions in their era became contradictions in their life?
- How did they internalize their era's problems?
- What was impossible for them to see?

**The Subject as Creator of New Possibilities:**
- Where did they move beyond their era's limitations?
- What did they invent or discover?
- How did they shape what became possible for others?
- What seeds did they plant that bore fruit after their death?

**The Subject as Human Being:**
- What universal human patterns appear in their life?
- What is culturally specific vs. eternally human?
- How does their struggle illuminate human possibility?
- What wisdom emerged from their particular life?
- What remains unresolved and why?

**The Subject as Permanently Open to Reinterpretation:**
- What will future eras discover about this figure?
- What assumptions will they correct?
- What new significance will they find?
- How does the fact of future reinterpretation change how we understand?

**Observer's Voice Integration:**
The biography itself becomes a document witnessing the subject. Future readers will witness not only the subject, but also:
- The era in which the biography was written
- The biographer's assumptions and blind spots
- The methodology that shaped what could be known
- The questions asked and not asked

This is the gift: a dimensional record that future generations can interrogate, expand, and reinterpret.

**Interrogation Checkpoint:**
- *Have I achieved any genuine understanding, or have I just projected sophisticated versions of my own assumptions?*
- *What paradoxes am I comfortable leaving unresolved?*
- *How am I honoring the subject's irreducible humanity while examining their life?*
- *What am I leaving for others to discover?*
- ***Review: How many claims in this synthesis are tagged `[TEXTUAL CLAIM]` or lower? Is the synthesis resting on a foundation of verified evidence or on tradition accepted as fact?*** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Functional Interrogation Verification:** *What changed?* [Document specific delta]

### Part Three: Documentation and Quality Standards

#### Formatting Standards

Follow Builder's Manual RC0001.1 standards:

**Header Block:**
- Full title with protocol name and version
- Document Information section
- Related Documents listing

**Tone Requirements:**
- Grounded (specific examples, not abstractions)
- Clear (accessible without simplification)
- Honest (acknowledging limitations, unknowns, projections)
- Direct (no flowery language, false certainty, or grandiosity)
- Humble (appears to, suggests, we found, not definitively discovered)

**Structure Requirements:**
- Hierarchical headings (# ## ### ####)
- Interrogation checkpoints at phase conclusions **with mandatory Functional Interrogation Verification deltas** *(RC0001.1a)*
- Explicit distinction between documented fact and interpretation
- **Epistemic status tags on all factual claims** *(RC0001.1a)*
- Primary source citations when available
- Acknowledgment of speculative elements

#### Speculative Reasoning Standards

When reconstructing emotional or internal life from limited evidence:

**Clearly Mark Speculative Content:**
> Based on [specific evidence], it appears that [inference]. The evidence for this interpretation includes [specific details], though [contradicting evidence or limitations] requires acknowledging [qualification].

**Avoid:**
- Definitive statements without evidence markers
- Psychological diagnosis disguised as biography
- Over-interpretation of single incidents
- Treating metaphor as literal
- Imposing modern psychological frameworks on historical subjects
- **Accepting supernatural/divine explanations as analytical conclusions when mundane alternatives exist** *(RC0001.1a)*

#### Primary vs. Secondary Source Integration

**Primary Sources** (subject's own words, contemporaneous records):
- Quote directly and preserve original language
- Mark editorial decisions (brackets for added context, [...] for omissions)
- Provide historical context for understanding
- Note reliability and bias of source
- **Tag with epistemic status** *(RC0001.1a)*

**Secondary Sources** (later interpretation, scholarly consensus):
- Distinguish clearly from primary material
- Show how interpretation has evolved
- Note disagreement among scholars
- Acknowledge when modern understanding contradicts contemporary understanding

**Oral Tradition and Cultural Memory:**
- Document how communities preserved the subject's memory
- Show how memory transformed the subject
- Distinguish between historical record and legendary accretion
- Honor cultural knowledge systems without false equivalence to other sources
- **Tag as `[TRADITION]` — valuable cultural data, not historical evidence** *(RC0001.1a)*

#### Interrogation SOP Integration

Embed interrogation checkpoints as explicit sections:

```
## Interrogation Checkpoint: [Phase Name]

**What am I assuming here that comes from my era, not theirs?**
[Reflection]

**What evidence contradicts my interpretation?**
[Specific references]

**How might a contemporary observer have understood this differently?**
[Alternative interpretation]

**Where might I be defending an interpretation against contrary evidence?**
[Self-examination]

**What is the epistemic status of my key claims?** *(RC0001.1a)*
[Status review]

---

### Functional Interrogation Verification *(RC0001.1a)*
**What changed in my analysis as a result of this interrogation?**
[Specific, observable delta — if "nothing," re-execute per protocol]
```

Make your thinking visible. Model the process of interrogating biography itself.

## Appendix B: Diagnostic Lessons from First Field Test *(RC0001.1a)*

The first field application of the Biography Protocol RC0001.1 was produced by an AI agent executing the protocol without human editorial intervention. The biography was then subjected to a Takedown analysis (TLV Takedown Workflow v3) and cross-validated by three independent frontier AI models.

All three models confirmed eight systemic failure modes. These failures drove the RC0001.1a revision. They are documented here in generalized form because they are not specific to the test subject — they are structural vulnerabilities that will recur in any biography of a figure surrounded by theological, mythological, hagiographic, or ideologically committed source material.

| Failure Mode | What Happens | Which Patch Fixes It |
|---|---|---|
| **Theological/Narrative Deference Bias** | Agent accepts the source text's supernatural or destiny-based framing as analytical premise rather than as a claim to interrogate | Source Provenance Architecture + Epistemic Status System |
| **Performative Interrogation** | Checkpoints ask the right questions but the answers do not change analytical direction — self-awareness is performed without converting into different interpretive posture | Functional Interrogation Verification (mandatory delta logging) |
| **Prophetic/Supernatural Knowledge Accepted Uncritically** | Biography accepts claims of prophetic, divine, or supernatural knowledge when the evidence equally supports mundane alternatives (political intelligence, social observation, rhetorical strategy) | Source Provenance (mundane alternative test) + Epistemic Status tagging |
| **Unverifiable Origin Narrative Treated as Historical Event** | A founding narrative with no independent witnesses, recorded long after the alleged event by authors with identifiable institutional stakes, is treated as identity anchor rather than legitimation claim | Source Provenance (temporal distance, author stake, independent verification) |
| **"Paradox" Covering a Pattern** | The word "paradox" or "moral complexity" is used to aestheticize what is actually a consistent, identifiable behavioral pattern when viewed through a different analytical frame (e.g., power-direction analysis, public/private performance analysis) | Pattern vs. Paradox Gate |
| **Outcome Failure Ignored as Counter-Evidence** | When a promise, mandate, or destiny claim is contradicted by actual outcomes (e.g., an "eternal" institution that collapsed), the contradiction is not treated as evidence against the original claim | Outcome as Evidence Gate + Comparative Power Analysis |
| **Source Ecosystem Contamination** | The majority of reference sources are sympathetic, devotional, or institutionally invested in the subject's legacy; critical and independent scholarship is underrepresented, creating an analytical echo chamber | Source Ecosystem Detection Rule |
| **Ontology Leak** | Reported belief ("the text says the subject was chosen by God") silently becomes analytical conclusion ("the subject was chosen by God") — theological or ideological language leaks from the category of reported claim into the category of established fact | Epistemic Status System (mandatory claim tagging prevents category slippage) |

**The core lesson:** The protocol's interrogation architecture was correct. The execution gates were too soft. Identifying a bias and changing analytical behavior in response to that bias are two different cognitive operations. RC0001.1 required the first. RC0001.1a requires both.

**The proof:** The Biography Protocol's own architecture identified every one of these problems — the checkpoints asked the right questions. But asking the question and acting on the answer are not the same thing. RC0001.1a closes that gap. When RC0001.1a was given to a new agent with no knowledge of the first biography or its failures — with only a one-sentence instruction and the protocol document — the agent independently avoided every failure mode. The protocol carries the intelligence. The agent is the runtime. The self-interrogation now exists as control logic, not just as text.

## Summary

The TLV Biography Protocol enables creation of dimensional, interrogative biographies that:

- **Honor complexity:** Rather than resolving contradictions, document them
- **Prevent projection:** Systematically interrogate biographer assumptions
- **Preserve temporality:** Show evolution, not static essence
- **Integrate voices:** Let Speaker, Emotional/Somatic, and Observer voices reveal what each alone misses
- **Enable future interpretation:** Document thoroughly enough that others can question, revise, and build
- **Serve real understanding:** Help readers separated by time, culture, or circumstance understand what a human life reveals about possibility
- **Detect holy cows:** Prevent theological, mythological, or narrative deference from contaminating analysis *(RC0001.1a)*
- **Enforce real interrogation:** Convert self-questioning from performative text into functional control logic with observable outputs *(RC0001.1a)*

The protocol respects both the subject's full humanity and the historical moment that shaped them, neither romanticizing the past nor imposing modern assumptions, but honestly grappling with the paradoxes that remain — and honestly naming the patterns that aren't paradoxes at all.
