**Category**: Remorse/Self-Reproach  
**Precision Level**: HIGH  
**Somatic Integration Required**: YES  
**Temporal Translation Required**: NO  
**Cultural Bridge Integration**: REQUIRED

#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Old English "gyltig" (liable, responsible)
- **Original Meaning**: Liable and responsible; bearing burden of wrongdoing and moral debt
- **Semantic Evolution**: Moral responsibility → wrongdoing burden → contemporary remorseful self-reproach and conscience weight
- **Cultural Shifts**: Moral responsibility → wrongdoing burden → remorseful self-reproach → forgiveness understanding
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - **Original Physical Meaning**: Body liable and responsible, bearing burden of wrongdoing and moral debt
  - **Embodiment Evolution**: Moral responsibility → wrongdoing burden → remorseful self-reproach
  - **Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Moral responsibility → wrongdoing burden → forgiveness development

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Feeling remorseful and self-reproaching; experiencing conscience weight and moral responsibility burden
- **Secondary Meanings**: Remorseful, ashamed, regretful, contrite, repentant, culpable
- **Colloquial Usage**: "Feeling guilty," "guilty conscience," "guilt about actions"
- **Register**: Negative; indicates remorse calling for forgiveness and conscience support
- **Frequency Trend**: Very high; fundamental for describing remorse and moral responsibility
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
  - **Body Awareness Trends**: Recognition of guilt as embodied remorse supported by forgiveness awareness and conscience healing
  - **Clinical Integration**: Guilt therapy and conscience processing with forgiveness development
  - **Cultural Disconnection**: May be toxic or overwhelming without guilt processing and forgiveness development
