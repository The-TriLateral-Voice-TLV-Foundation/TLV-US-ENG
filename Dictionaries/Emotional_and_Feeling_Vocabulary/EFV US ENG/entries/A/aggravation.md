#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "aggravatio" (act of making heavier) from "aggravare"
- **Original Meaning**: The process of making something heavier or more burdensome
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical burdening → problem worsening → emotional irritation source
- **Cultural Shifts**: Medical terminology → legal concept → everyday annoyance
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Process of adding weight or burden to something
  - Embodiment Evolution**: Physical loading → emotional burdening → irritation experience
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Medical symptom worsening → emotional state description

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: The act of making something worse; a source of irritation or annoyance
- **Secondary Meanings**: Annoyance, irritation, worsening, exacerbation, provocation
- **Colloquial Usage**: "Traffic is such an aggravation," "aggravation of symptoms"
- **Register**: Neutral; used in both medical and casual contexts
- **Frequency Trend**: Stable; useful for describing both problem sources and emotional states
