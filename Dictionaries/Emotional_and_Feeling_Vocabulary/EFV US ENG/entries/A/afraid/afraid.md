### AFRAID

**Category**: Fear/Anxiety  
**Precision Level**: HIGH  
**Somatic Integration Required**: YES  
**Temporal Translation Required**: NO

#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Old English "afrædan" from "a-" (intensive) + "frǣdan" (to endanger)
- **Original Meaning**: To put in danger, frighten, or cause to feel fear
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical endangerment → emotional state of fear → contemporary anxiety
- **Cultural Shifts**: Survival-based fear → moral/spiritual fear → psychological condition
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Body response to danger—trembling, alertness, protective posturing
  - Embodiment Evolution: Immediate threat response → chronic anxiety patterns
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Physical danger reactions → psychological fear responses

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Feeling fear or apprehension about potential danger, pain, or trouble
- **Secondary Meanings**: Scared, anxious, worried, apprehensive, concerned, fearful
- **Colloquial Usage**: "I'm afraid of heights," "afraid to ask," "I'm afraid so"
- **Register**: Universal; used across all contexts from casual to formal
- **Frequency Trend**: Stable; fundamental emotion word in all languages
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
  - Body Awareness Trends: Recognized in trauma therapy as embodied fear response
  - Clinical Integration: Central to anxiety disorder treatment and fear processing
  - Cultural Disconnection: Shame around fear expression in some cultures
