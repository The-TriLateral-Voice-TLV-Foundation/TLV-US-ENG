#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: French "alarme" from Italian "all'arme" (to arms!) - military call to arms
- **Original Meaning**: Warning call to prepare for battle; call to weapons
- **Semantic Evolution**: Military warning → general danger signal → emotional response to threat
- **Cultural Shifts**: Military command → community warning → individual fear response
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Physical preparation for battle - grabbing weapons, alert stance
  - Embodiment Evolution**: Combat readiness → alert attention → anxiety response
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Military mobilization → civilian alert response → anxiety activation
