#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "adōrāre" from "ad-" (to) + "ōrāre" (to speak, pray)
- **Original Meaning**: To worship through prayer; to regard as divine
- **Semantic Evolution**: Religious worship → deep reverence → intense love → casual strong liking
- **Cultural Shifts**: Sacred worship → romantic devotion → contemporary enthusiastic affection
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Prayer gestures and worship postures directed toward divine
  - Embodiment Evolution: Sacred devotion → intimate tenderness → enthusiastic appreciation
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Ritual worship → personal devotion → casual affection expression

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage

- **Secondary Meanings**: Worship, revere, love intensely, be devoted to, cherish
- **Colloquial Usage**: "I adore chocolate," "I adore you," "absolutely adore"
- **Register**: Intimate to casual; can be light or profound depending on context
