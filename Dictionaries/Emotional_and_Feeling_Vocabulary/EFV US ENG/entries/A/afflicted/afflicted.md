#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "afflīctus" from "afflīgere" (to strike down, damage) from "ad-" (to) + "flīgere" (to strike)
- **Original Meaning**: Struck down, damaged, or beaten down by force
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical striking → suffering under hardship → being burdened by misfortune
- **Cultural Shifts**: Divine punishment → medical condition → contemporary struggle with adversity
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Physical impact and damage from external force
  - Embodiment Evolution**: Physical striking → emotional/spiritual burden → embodied suffering
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Divine judgment → medical suffering → integrated mind-body distress

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Suffering from pain, distress, or hardship; burdened by difficulty
- **Secondary Meanings**: Troubled, distressed, burdened, suffering, tormented, pained
- **Colloquial Usage**: "Afflicted with illness," "afflicted by grief," "the afflicted"
- **Register**: Formal/literary; often medical or religious contexts
- **Frequency Trend**: Declining in casual use; maintained in medical/religious contexts
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:

  - Clinical Integration**: Important in pain management and chronic condition treatment
