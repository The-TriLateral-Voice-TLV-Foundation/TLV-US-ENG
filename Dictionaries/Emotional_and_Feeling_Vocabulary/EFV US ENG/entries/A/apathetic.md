#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Greek "apathētikos" from "a-" (without) + "pathos" (feeling, suffering)
- **Original Meaning**: Without feeling or emotion; indifferent to suffering
- **Semantic Evolution**: Philosophical ideal → emotional numbness → contemporary disengagement
- **Cultural Shifts**: Stoic virtue → pathological condition → modern disconnection phenomenon
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Absence of physical emotional response and bodily reactivity
  - Embodiment Evolution: Philosophical detachment → emotional numbness → disconnected presence
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Ideal emotional control → problematic disconnection → clinical concern

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern; indifferent
- **Secondary Meanings**: Indifferent, uninterested, dispassionate, emotionally flat, disconnected
- **Colloquial Usage**: "Apathetic attitude," "feeling apathetic," "apathetic about politics"
- **Register**: Formal to neutral; often indicates concerning emotional state
- **Frequency Trend**: Increasing; relevant to modern phenomena of disconnection and disengagement
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
  - Body Awareness Trends: Recognized as embodied disconnection requiring re-engagement practices
  - Clinical Integration: Symptom in depression, trauma, and dissociative conditions
  - Cultural Disconnection: May be normalized without understanding underlying emotional needs
