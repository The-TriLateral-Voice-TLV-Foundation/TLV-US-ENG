#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: German "Ambivalenz" coined by Eugen Bleuler (1910) from Latin "ambi-" (both) + "valentia" (strength)
- **Original Meaning**: Simultaneous existence of opposing feelings or attitudes
- **Semantic Evolution**: Psychological term → general emotional conflict → contemporary indecisiveness
- **Cultural Shifts**: Clinical psychology → popular psychology → everyday emotional literacy
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Two opposing forces creating internal tension
  - Embodiment Evolution: Psychological concept → embodied experience of internal conflict
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Clinical observation → personal emotional awareness

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something
- **Secondary Meanings**: Conflicted, uncertain, torn, undecided, having mixed emotions
- **Colloquial Usage**: "I feel ambivalent about the decision," "ambivalent feelings"
- **Register**: Formal to neutral; indicates complex emotional state
- **Frequency Trend**: Increasing; growing emotional literacy and complexity awareness
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
