#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "adōrātiō" from "adōrāre" (to speak to, worship) from "ad-" (to) + "ōrāre" (to speak, pray)
- **Original Meaning**: Religious worship; speaking prayers to divine beings
- **Semantic Evolution**: Divine worship → deep reverence → intense love with reverence quality
- **Cultural Shifts**: Religious devotion → romantic idealization → contemporary intense affection
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Physical prostration and prayer postures in worship
  - Embodiment Evolution: Religious reverence → romantic devotion → integrated love-awe response
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Ritual worship postures → intimate reverence gestures

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Intense love combined with deep respect and reverence
- **Secondary Meanings**: Deep devotion, worship, idealization, profound affection
- **Colloquial Usage**: "I adore you," "filled with adoration," "mutual adoration"
- **Register**: Intimate to formal; indicates very strong positive feeling
- **Frequency Trend**: Stable in intimate contexts; maintained religious usage
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
