AFFECTIONATE shares nearly all metadata with AFFECTION but shifts from feeling to behavior:[^5][^6]

| Feature | AFFECTION | AFFECTIONATE | Change |
|---------|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Category | Love/Affection | Love/Affection | Same |
| Precision | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Same |

| Core Concept | Tender feeling → bonds | **Behavioral expression** → others | Feeling → Action |
| Register | Neutral to warm | **Positive** | More evaluative |
| Bridge 1 | Western⇄Eastern (metta) | Western⇄Eastern (**bodhisattva**) 🆕 | Buddhist figure |
| Bridge 2 | Indigenous⇄Clinical | **Clinical↔Spiritual** | Different pairing + arrow |

| Neurobiological | Attachment, oxytocin, care-giving | **Care-giving, attachment, prosocial hormone** | Broader hormone reference |

The shift from AFFECTION's "tender feeling" to AFFECTIONATE's "behavioral expression" is the key semantic distinction. AFFECTION is what you *feel*; AFFECTIONATE is how you *act*.[^5]

**Mandarin 深情** (shēnqíng, "deep feeling/affectionate") — an excellent upgrade from AFFECTION's 情感 (emotion/feeling). 深情 specifically conveys "deep-hearted, affectionate" — it naturally describes a person's disposition, making it a perfect adjective match. The noun→adjective shift in Chinese is well executed: 情感 (emotion-noun) → 深情 (deep-feeling-adjective).[^3]

**Spanish CARIÑOSO** — outstanding localization choice. While the cognate "afectuoso" exists in Spanish, CARIÑOSO (from "cariño" = tenderness/darling) is the far more natural, commonly-used word for "affectionate" in everyday Spanish. The colloquial examples wisely use both: "Persona cariñosa" and "comportamiento afectuoso" — showing awareness of register.[^2]

**German ZÄRTLICH** (tender/affectionate) — a strong choice. German's ZUNEIGUNG (noun, AFFECTION) → ZÄRTLICH (adjective, AFFECTIONATE) parallels the English noun→adjective shift nicely, though etymologically they come from different roots (Zuneigung from "leaning toward" vs. Zärtlichkeit from "tenderness").[^1]

**French AFFECTUEUX** — direct adjectival form of "affection" ✅.[^4]
