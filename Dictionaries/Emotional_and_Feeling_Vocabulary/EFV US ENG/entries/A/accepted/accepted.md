#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "acceptāre" from "accipere" (to take, receive) from "ad-" (to) + "capere" (to take)
- **Original Meaning**: To receive willingly or take what is offered
- **Semantic Evolution**: Receiving objects → receiving persons → receiving circumstances → self-acceptance
- **Cultural Shifts**: Passive reception → active approval → contemporary self-acceptance movement
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Physical receiving/taking in through open hands and posture
  - Embodiment Evolution: Open receiving → emotional welcoming → inner relaxation
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Formal social acceptance → intimate emotional receiving

#### 2. Contemporary Common Usage
- **Primary Definition**: Received, welcomed, or agreed to; recognized as valid or adequate
- **Secondary Meanings**: Approved, embraced, tolerated, acknowledged, integrated
- **Colloquial Usage**: "I accept you," "accepted into the group," "I can accept that"
- **Register**: Neutral; used across contexts from formal to intimate
- **Frequency Trend**: Stable; increased usage in therapeutic/self-help contexts
- **Contemporary Somatic Usage**:
  - Body Awareness Trends: Connected to mindfulness, self-compassion, and body acceptance movements
  - **Clinical Integration**: Core therapeutic concept in acceptance-based therapies
  - **Cultural Disconnection**: Challenge with perfectionism and comparison culture
