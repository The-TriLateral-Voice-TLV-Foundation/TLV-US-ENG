### 🔔 Mandarin Terminology Shift: 推荐 vs. 建议

The Mandarin translation uses **推荐** (tuījiàn) for RECOMMENDED, whereas ALL prior entries used **建议** (jiànyì). Both mean "recommend," but:[^7][^8][^9][^4]
- 建议 = suggest/advise (softer, more advisory)
- 推荐 = recommend (stronger, more endorsing)

This is a minor inconsistency — not wrong, but a terminology drift from the established pattern. Similarly, the translation note uses slightly different phrasing than prior entries.

### 🔔 Mandarin Somatic Terminology Shift: 躯体 vs. 身体

More significantly, AFFECTION's Mandarin translation uses **躯体** (qūtǐ, "soma/body") throughout where ALL prior entries used **身体** (shēntǐ, "body"):[^4]

| Term | Prior 10 entries | AFFECTION |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| "Somatic Integration" | 身体整合 | **躯体整合** |
| "Somatic Etymology" | 身体词源 | **躯体词源** |
| "Somatic Markers" | 身体标记 | **躯体标记** |
| "Somatic Signature" | 身体特征 | **躯体特征** |

**Analysis**: 躯体 is actually the more technically precise term — it corresponds directly to the medical/clinical "somatic" (as in 躯体化 = somatization). 身体 is the everyday word for "body." The shift to 躯体 is arguably an *improvement* in precision, but creates a cross-entry inconsistency. **Recommendation**: Standardize on one or the other across the full lexicon.
