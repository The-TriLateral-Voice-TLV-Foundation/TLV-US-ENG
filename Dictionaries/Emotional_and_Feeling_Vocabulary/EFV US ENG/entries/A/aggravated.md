- **Root Language**: Latin "aggravatus" from "aggravare" (to make heavy) from "ad-" (to) + "gravis" (heavy)
- **Original Meaning**: To make heavier or more burdensome; to worsen
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical weight → emotional burden → intensified irritation
- **Cultural Shifts**: Medical terminology → legal language → everyday emotional expression
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Adding weight or burden, making something heavier
