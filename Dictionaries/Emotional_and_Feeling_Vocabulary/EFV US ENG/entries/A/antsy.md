#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: American English, from "having ants in one's pants" (restless, unable to sit still)
- **Original Meaning**: Physical restlessness as if ants were crawling on skin
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical metaphor → psychological restlessness → contemporary impatience
- **Cultural Shifts**: Folk expression → casual emotional vocabulary → recognized restlessness state
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Sensation of crawling, itching, needing to move constantly
  - Embodiment Evolution: Physical crawling sensation → restless energy → impatient agitation
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts: Folk body metaphor → psychological restlessness recognition

- **Colloquial Usage**: "Feeling antsy," "getting antsy," "antsy to leave"
