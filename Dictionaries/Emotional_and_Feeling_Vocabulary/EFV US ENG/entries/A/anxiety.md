- **Root Language**: Latin "anxietas" (distress, anxiety) from "anxius" (distressed, troubled)
- **Original Meaning**: State of distress, trouble, or choking sensation
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical distress state → emotional condition → psychological disorder → contemporary clinical category
- **Cultural Shifts**: Physical ailment → moral weakness → nervous condition → treatable medical condition
