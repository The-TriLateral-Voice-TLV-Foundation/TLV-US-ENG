#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "absorbēre" from "ab-" (away) + "sorbēre" (to suck in)
- **Original Meaning**: Physical process of liquid being drawn into material (like sponge absorption)
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical absorption → mental concentration → complete engagement
- **Cultural Shifts**: Mechanical process → psychological state → contemporary "flow state"
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning: Material drawing in and containing liquid completely
  - Embodiment Evolution**: Mental attention "drinking in" experience → full engagement
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Physical metaphor → psychological immersion → mindfulness practice
