#### 1. Etymology & Historical Development
- **Root Language**: Latin "agitatus" from "agitare" (to set in motion, drive) from "agere" (to drive, do)
- **Original Meaning**: Set in motion, stirred up, driven to movement
- **Semantic Evolution**: Physical stirring → emotional disturbance → contemporary restlessness
- **Cultural Shifts**: Mechanical agitation → psychological disturbance → clinical symptom
- **Somatic Etymology**:
  - Original Physical Meaning**: Physical stirring or shaking, setting in motion
  - Embodiment Evolution**: Physical movement → emotional restlessness → nervous system activation
  - Cultural Somatic Shifts**: Mechanical motion → psychological agitation → therapeutic target
