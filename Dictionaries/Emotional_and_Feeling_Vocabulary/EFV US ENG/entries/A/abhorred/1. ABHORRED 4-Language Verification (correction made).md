## Issues Found

### 1. German: Stray Markdown Bold Marker (Section 3)

In the German version, Section 3 contains a misplaced `**` bold marker on the "Verkörperte Authentizität" line:[^4]

```
- **Verkörperte Authentizität**: Ganzkörper-Ablehnungsreaktion
```

The `**` appears to close **after** the colon rather than wrapping the label text properly. This would cause incorrect Markdown rendering.

**Severity**: Low — affects display only, not content.

### 2. German: TLV Voice Names Use Plural "Stimmen"

The German translation uses plural forms — "Sprecherstimmen" (Speaker voices), "Emotionale Stimmen" (Emotional voices), "Beobachterstimmen" (Observer voices) — where the English uses singular "Voice". While grammatically natural in German compound nouns, singular "Stimme" (e.g., Sprecherstimme, Emotionale Stimme, Beobachterstimme) would better mirror the English TLV brand terminology.[^4]

**Severity**: Very low — stylistic preference for brand consistency.

### 3. Mandarin: Arrow Symbol Difference

The Mandarin version uses ↔ (simple double arrow) in the Cultural Bridge examples, while the English, French, Spanish, and German versions all use ⇄ (equilibrium arrow). Functionally identical in meaning, but a minor visual inconsistency across the translation set.[^2]

**Severity**: Negligible.
